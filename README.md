Game concept. Asteroids, kept simple but with very high end rendering and auto-camera effects.  The twist: its impossible to cross the asteroid field unless played two player. Two enemies trapped in the field, they can ONLY survive by protecting each other to escape - and if they can't escape both factions are doomed - everywhere.


NEUTRAL ZERO is live. Here's what's in it:
The impossible-alone mechanic: The exit portal only activates when both ships occupy it simultaneously. One ship can clear a path, but a solo ship can never escape — the game is structurally cooperative, not just strategically.
Auto-camera system: The camera follows the midpoint between both ships and dynamically zooms based on their separation distance — pulling out wide when you split apart, tightening as you converge. Screen shake on impacts, smooth lerped follow.
Allied Shield: When ships fly within ~300px of each other, a dashed gradient beam connects them, aura rings pulse around each hull, and both take 28% less asteroid damage. Staying together is mechanically rewarded.
Rendering layers: Star parallax (3 layers), nebula washes, glowing asteroid polygons with damage-crack indicators, engine plumes with flicker, ship trails that fade, bullet trails, particle explosions, vignette, and the pulsing golden exit portal.
The narrative: Win → the war ends that day. Lose → it continues forty more years. The stakes are built into the splash screens.
